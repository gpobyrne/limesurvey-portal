UHL CSAR-AI Study

Last updated: 15 June 2026

Study title A Comparative Study of the Accuracy and Reliability of Artificial Intelligence Large Language Models Versus Human Healthcare Workers in the Interpretation of Handwritten Medical Notes (CSAR-AI).
Study Ref 0392
Ethical Status Approved by UHL Research Ethics Committee, 26 January 2026.

This portal provides access to the UHL CSAR-AI Study Human Healthcare Worker Survey, which runs on a custom instance of the open-source application LimeSurvey .

The aim of this study is to assess the performance of a GDPR-compliant AI model, which we have trained to read and interpret handwritten clinical notes. We fine-tuned this model by exposing it to thousands of anonymised excerpts from clinical notes written by healthcare workers at UHL.

We would like to compare the accuracy of this model against that of human healthcare workers, in order to evaluate whether this type of AI-assisted interpretation may have a role in improving the readability, interpretation, and safe handling of handwritten clinical documentation.

Privacy and data

All respondent data will remain inside the secured LimeSurvey hosting environment. Only data within the scope of the current study will be collected. Any export, backup, retention, or deletion of participant data will be conducted in a manner compliant with European GDPR legislation and local data retention policies .

This landing page is hosted via a publicically-accessible GitHub repository ; which is used to serve the website frontend only. This repository will not be used to host respondent data, participant email lists, exported survey responses, database credentials, or private configuration files.
